Who Am I

  • Humble Beginnings

    Initially when I entered the University of California, Irvine I wasn't sure what I wanted to do and programming seemed like a far fetched idea. As such, I continued to go to school for Business/Economics and eventually got my degree for that major. I returned home to San Diego shortly after and began working at a local company, San Diego Sol, providing them with marketing help as well as managing projects.

  • A Programmer is Born

    After working at San Diego Sol for some time I was eventually tasked with making some changes and updating the company website. Though the modifications were relatively small in nature I immediately took interest in the idea of creating, updating, and modifying websites and began to teach myself about it in more depth. Through various online courses and experimentation on my computer, my interest for working with websites turned more into a passion which eventually lead my LearningFuze, an accelerated web development program. Throughout the course and over 1000 hours spent learning, practicing, and coding, LearningFuze has equipped me with the knowledge and ability to code efficiently and tackle problems from a logical, simplified standpoint.

  • Transitioning to Work

    I now aim to bring my knowledge and experience to a position in which I can help contribute to a team of individuals working on a larger project. My end goal would be to continue gaining experience in the field of web development and to find a position in which I can effectively play my role as a software developer.
